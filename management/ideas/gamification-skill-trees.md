# Gamification: Skill Trees

## Core Concept
Skyrim-style skill trees where each tradition has its own fully independent progression path. The app surfaces the hidden developmental curriculum that already exists within each tradition, making it visible and trackable.

## Design Principles
- **No cross-tree dependencies.** You never need level 3 in yoga to unlock level 5 in Tanya. Each tree is fully self-contained and completable on its own.
- **User chooses what to engage with.** Pick one tree or multiple — no judgment, no pressure.
- **RPG build mentality.** Every path is a valid "build." Pure Tanya is a paladin. Pure body work is a tank. Mixing traditions is a battle mage. No build is better or worse.
- **Struggle is rewarded, not penalised.** XP comes from engaging the practice, not from perfection. Falling off and coming back is an integrated mechanic, not a failure state.
- **Tradition-pure content.** Each tree uses its own language, its own framework, its own metaphysics. No blending or syncretism imposed by the system.

## B2B vs B2C Tension
This feature is firmly B2C — it's the app having opinions about progression and presenting that to individual users. The B2B side (teachers building content) could potentially feed *into* skill trees (teacher creates a meditation, tags what it trains, platform handles the progression layer), but the gamification itself is a direct-to-practitioner feature. These two models don't need to be resolved now — just be aware they pull in different directions.

## Example Trees

### Benoni (Tanya / Chabad)
Based on Likutei Amarim by Rabbi Schneur Zalman of Liadi. The Benoni's path is about mastery and governance of expression — never about transforming the underlying nature. The animal soul remains active; you master the three "garments" (thought, speech, action) through which it expresses.

**Branches:**

#### 1. Thought (Machshavah) — Ch. 12, 26-28
The hardest garment to control. The Benoni's primary battlefield.
1. Awareness of intrusion — recognising when a foreign thought has entered (Ch. 26: this arising is normal, not failure)
2. Non-engagement — "it arose and fell" (alah b'da'ato vs machshiv b'da'ato, Ch. 12)
3. Active redirection — "pushing away with the left hand," gentle but firm (Ch. 28)
4. Substitution through contemplation (Hitbonenut) — replacing content, not just emptying (Ch. 41-44)
5. Sustained clean occupancy — extended periods with the thought garment fully occupied by the divine soul

#### 2. Speech (Dibbur) — Ch. 12, 15
Intermediate difficulty. Easier than thought, harder than action.
1. Restraint from harmful speech
2. Restraint from idle speech (devarim beteilim, Ch. 7-8)
3. Intentional sacred speech — directing speech toward Torah, prayer, kindness
4. Prayer as speech practice — kavanah in tefillah (Ch. 38-39)
5. Speech as channel — dirah b'tachtonim through sanctified speech (Ch. 37)

#### 3. Action (Ma'aseh) — Ch. 12, 15, 35-37
The most accessible garment. Where the Benoni begins.
1. Basic ethical conduct — refraining from prohibited actions
2. Consistent mitzvah observance — the 248 positive commandments as "limbs" of the soul (Ch. 23)
3. Hiddur mitzvah — performing with care and beauty beyond the minimum (Ch. 35)
4. Tzedakah as paradigmatic action — equivalent to all other commandments combined (Ch. 37)
5. Action as transformation of the material — elevating the "sparks" in physical reality (Ch. 35-37)

#### 4. Generation of Emotions (Middot) — Ch. 3, 12-17, 38-44
The heart of the Benoni's inner work.
1. Hidden love revealed (ahavah mesuteret) — uncovering the innate inheritance (Ch. 18-19)
2. Intellectually generated love — through Hitbonenut; "as water reflects a face" (Ch. 43-44)
3. Awe and reverence (yirah tata'ah) — fear of separation, not punishment; the "gate" to all practice (Ch. 41-43)
4. Fiery longing (re'uta d'liba) — intense love that comes and goes; peaks during prayer, fades after (Ch. 9, 16)
5. Joy in service (simcha shel mitzvah) — joy from performing God's will, even when it feels mechanical; "even if like a donkey" (Ch. 26-31, 33)

#### 5. Intellectual Faculties (Mochin — ChaBaD) — Ch. 3, 14, 42-44
The command centre. The engine of the Benoni's entire system.
1. Chochmah — initial flash of insight; the "koach mah" (power of "what?"); self-nullification before ego structures the perception
2. Binah — sustained contemplation; Hitbonenut is literally "Binah-based" (same root); developing the flash into a chain of understanding
3. Da'at — deep knowing / attachment; the bridge that transfers intellectual content into emotional impact (Ch. 3, 42)
4. Moach shalit al halev — mind rules the heart; the defining skill of the Benoni; constitutional authority of intellect over emotion (Ch. 12, 14, 17)
5. Bittul ha-yesh — nullification of self; the ego recognises itself as secondary to the divine reality (Ch. 35, 46-48)

#### 6. Lovingkindness / Ahavas Yisrael — Ch. 32
1. Basic kindness and non-harm
2. Recognising the shared soul root — all souls share a common root; bodies separate, souls one (Ch. 32)
3. Ahavat Yisrael — love of fellow as self; identifying with soul rather than body (Ch. 32)
4. Love despite difference — rejecting behaviour while loving the soul (Ch. 32)
5. Selfless giving — outward expression of inner bittul; when the "I" is diminished, giving becomes natural (Ch. 32, 37)

**Sefirot as Attribute Stats:**
The ten soul-faculties can function as a parallel stat system underlying the branches. Advancing certain branches requires minimum levels in certain sefirot:
- Chesed (Lovingkindness) — drives Lovingkindness branch + Action branch
- Gevurah (Discipline) — powers restraint nodes in Thought and Speech
- Tiferet (Harmony) — mid-tier emotional attainment; integrates love and awe
- Netzach (Endurance) — persistence across all branches when motivation fades
- Hod (Humility) — prerequisite for advanced love; supports Bittul
- Yesod (Foundation) — keystone; unlocks when multiple branches reach mid-level
- Malchut (Sovereignty) — final expression; the Benoni's impact on the world

**Key Milestones (unlockable achievements):**
- "The Matter Is Very Close" (Ki Karov) — Ch. 17. Realising Benoni mastery is genuinely accessible.
- "Push Away With the Left Hand" — Ch. 28. Gentle but firm thought redirection.
- "Moach Shalit Al HaLev" — Ch. 12, 14. Mind rules the heart. THE Benoni skill.
- "Simcha Shel Mitzvah" — Ch. 26-31. Joy from service regardless of subjective feeling.
- "Dirah B'Tachtonim" — Ch. 33, 36-37. Capstone. Making the physical world a dwelling place for the divine.
- Teshuvah — available as a "recovery mechanic." Falling off practice grants structured re-entry with its own rewards, reflecting "where ba'alei teshuvah stand, even the perfectly righteous cannot stand."

**What makes this distinctly Benoni (not Tzaddik):**
- The animal soul is never transformed — it remains fully active
- The three garments are controlled, not the essence
- Attainments during prayer must be re-achieved; every session is a fresh engagement
- High-level perks should "glow" during active practice and "dim" during lapses
- The struggle itself has unique value the Tzaddik cannot replicate (Ch. 27, Zohar)

### Body Control (Yoga)
Structured developmental path through physical mastery. Solves the problem that most people just repeat flows they like with no visible progression.

**Possible branches:**
1. Proprioception — awareness of what your body is actually doing
2. Isolation — engaging one muscle group without recruiting everything around it
3. Stability under load — holding control in challenging positions
4. Controlled transitions — moving between positions with precision, not momentum
5. Integration — full kinetic chains working together with conscious awareness

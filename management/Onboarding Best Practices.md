# Onboarding Best Practices
## Notes
1. Know thy user. (Define User Types)
	- Understand exactly who we want and who we don't want, (technical ability, flexibility preferences, motivation, etc ).
2. Work backwards. (Define what success looks like for you user)
	- Different users and user types have different goals and levels of experience and that changes as they go on their journey.
	- What steps absolutely need to help them to get them to success?
	- What is the simplest, most intuitive way to guide them through those steps.
	- How can you get them to their first win quickly?
	- Ideally not to many questions maybe with a skip and come back later to allow users to redo their onboarding.
3. Align with the business. (Align onboarding with the business model and strategy)
	- The best onboarding isn't about locking users in, but about showing them why they would never want to leave. 
4. Test and Tweak (Changes in small steps to optimise the experience)
	- If users drop off at a certain step, it doesn't mean that everything's ruined. It just means that something has gone wrong up to that point, which means that you should make small little changes and measure their effects.
		-  reduce the number of clicks.
		-  change the copy.
		- add a visual cue.
5. Common Mistakes:
	- Too many steps the best apps get users to their first win as fast as possible.
	- The wall of text problem 
	-  no escapes: Let the users learn as much or as little as they want in the onboarding process. This aligns with the idea of giving them access to it later on when they might understand more what it means.

# User profiles
## Qualities
### Creation Appetite
1. Full Creators - people who want to build their own exercises and programmes. ^full-creators
2. Curators - people who want to build their own programmes with prebuilt exercises. ^curators
3. Passive Adopters - people who want something that is prebuilt and works and does not want to change anything. ^passive-adopters
### Technical Ability
1. Senior Engineers - Fully understands [[Variables in Meditation Pro|Variables]] and [[Advanced Segments in Meditation Pro|Advanced Segments]] and is able to build [[Exercises in Meditation Pro|Exercises]] and [[Programmes in Meditation Pro|Programmes]] quickly and well. ^senior-engineers
2. Junior Engineers - Has a decent understanding of [[Variables in Meditation Pro|Variables]] and some [[Advanced Segments in Meditation Pro|Advanced Segments]] and is able to build any possible [[Exercises in Meditation Pro|Exercise]] or [[Programmes in Meditation Pro|Programme]] but its not easy and quick. ^junior-engineers
3. Vibe Coders - Has some understanding of [[Variables in Meditation Pro|Variables]], and [[Basic Segments in Meditation Pro|Basic Segments]] and is able builder some [[Exercises in Meditation Pro|Exercises]] and [[Programmes in Meditation Pro|Programmes]] but is likely to get stuck. ^vibe-coders
4. Non technical - Has almost no understanding of [[Variables in Meditation Pro|Variables]] and [[Segments in Meditation Pro|Segments]] and is only really able to hit play on prebuilt [[Exercises in Meditation Pro|Exercises]] and [[Programmes in Meditation Pro|Programmes]] or maybe tweak very obvious [[Variables in Meditation Pro|Variables]]. ^non-technical
### Gamification Preference
1. Max Geek - Wants to see exactly how far they've gone, get clear rewards for each step and have a clear character build. ^max-geek
2. Gamer - Want to have some feelings of success but is happy to go with the flow. ^gamer
3. Non-Gamer - Feels like xp, rewards and ranks cheapen spirituality and does not want any gamification. ^non-gamer
### Transparency Preference
1. Type A - Needs every detail of where they have come from, where they are, and where they're going, and how they'll get there. ^type-a
2. Chill planner -  Wants to know roughly where they're going, but doesn't need every detail. ^chill-planner
3. Moment Liver - Doesn't need to, need or want to know where they're going, just wants to do whatever's up today. ^moment-liver
### Attitude Towards Tradition
1. The purist - Has their tradition that they already fully believe in and doesn't want anything outside of that. ^the-purist
2. The grounded explorer - Has their main tradition that they want to do most of the time but might occasionally like to explore something else. ^the-grounded-explorer
3. The eclectic explorer - Is open to all traditions and is open to exploring everything. ^the-eclectic-explorer
### Experience Level
1. Black Belt - Has seriously meditated for 5+ years. ^black-belt
2. Amateur - Has done some amounts of serious consistent practice for a few years. ^amateur
3. White Belt - Has done some practice here and there. ^white-belt
4. Freshman -  Has never done any practice before. ^freshman
### Commitment Level
1. The monk - Is fully committed to serious daily practise. ^the-monk
2. The hobbyist -  is committed to doing some practise a couple days a week. ^the-hobbyist
3. The toe-dipper -  wants to take a look around but isn't really ready to commit to anything. ^the-toe-dipper
### Content used (Multiple allowed but at least 1)
1. Public content - browsing and using the public library. ^public-content
2. Private content - using content created by/for them. ^private-content
### Who manages you (Multiple allowed but at least 1)
1. By another person, ^managed-by-person
2. By the app. ^managed-by-app
3. By yourself. ^managed-by-self
### How you are managed 
1. Fully managed - told to do X on Y days and someone checks how you got on with that. ^fully-managed
2. Medium Control - want to get certain skills and would like someone to check up on them occasionally. ^medium-control-coachee
3. No Control - wants to explore at will with no management. ^no-control-coachee
### Who you manage  if you do (Multiple allowed but optional)
1. To Clients - They have their own body of clients that they have a personal relationship with that they're going to coach individually. ^to-clients
2. To Community - They have a community of people that trust them that they want to provide content for, but they don't have a personal relationship with all of the members. ^to-community
### How you manage if you do (1 per coachee)
1. Full Control - want students to do X on Y days and check how they got on with that. ^full-control
2. Medium Control - want students to get certain skills and might check up on them but not gonna be managing much. ^medium-control
3. No Control - want students to have access but aren't interested in directing what they do. ^no-control

# Guided meditation app — progressive exercises, AI voices, and Jewish music with [host]
## Summary
### The App: Core Problem and Solution
- Guided meditation content is expensive to produce at scale
  - A single 10-minute recording takes significant studio time
  - Progressive overload programs (building day by day) multiply that cost fast
  - A 5-min/day month-long program = \~2.5 hours of raw recording
- Solution: script-based system with variables (e.g. breath duration, loop count)
  - Variables defined upfront; audio generated or recorded per segment, not per session
  - Segments stitched together like video game dialogue, not recorded as one long take
  - AI voices available, but recording your own voice is fully supported
  - AI also optional for script drafting: use it or write by hand
### Demo Walkthrough
- Box breathing script shown: “breathe in slowly through your nose for \[phase duration\] seconds”
  - Phase duration is a variable, controls both narration and pause length
  - One recording session covers box breathing from 1 to 10 minutes via looping
- Thought control exercise shown: five progressive stages, each building on the last
  - User sets duration per stage, builds up incrementally before advancing
- Therapist/coach view: assign programs to clients, track progress on a calendar
  - Could work for private coaching (individual programs) or community content creators

- Strong shared interest in Jewish meditation as a specific niche
  - Music identified as the most underrated element: nigunim, fast/slow, varied styles

  - Exercises are precise but hard to self-direct without a guided structure
  - Nigun as a closing segment (post-practice) is a natural fit for the app

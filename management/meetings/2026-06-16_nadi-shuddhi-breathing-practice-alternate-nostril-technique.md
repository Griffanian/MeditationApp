# Nadi Shuddhi breathing practice — alternate nostril technique
### Summary
### What Nadi Shuddhi Is

- First in a series of three breathing practices
- Sanskrit: Nadi (energy channel) + Shuddhi (cleansing)
- Purpose: cleanse energy channels, balance mind and body
- Progression over coming weeks:
  1. Nadi Shuddhi (now)
  2. Anulom Vilom (advanced)
  3. Pranashanti (most advanced)

### How the Nostrils Work

- Left nostril activates the parasympathetic nervous system (rest and digest, yin, Chandra/moon)
- Right nostril activates the sympathetic nervous system (action, fight or flight, yang, Surya/sun)
- Goal: balance both systems through alternating breath
- Clinical use: energizes those who are depressed, calms those who are anxious

### The Technique

- Hand position: Nasika Mudra
  - Separate first two fingers from last two
  - Rest two fingers above the eyebrow
  - Thumb blocks one nostril, two fingers block the other
- Breathing pattern:
  1. Block right nostril, inhale through left
  2. Switch: block left, exhale through right
  3. Inhale through right (same nostril)
  4. Switch: block right, exhale through left
  5. Repeat
- Key rule: always switch on a full breath, not mid-exhale

### Practice Guidelines

- Start at 3 to 5 minutes per session
- Work up to 10, 15, or 20 minutes over time
- One nostril being more blocked than the other is normal (Swara Yoga)
- After finishing: sit quietly, notice the stillness, observe the breath slow down
- Even one minute produces noticeable calm
